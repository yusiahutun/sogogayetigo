A woman who wrongly claimed Ne-Yo was the father of her child is suing the star for slating her on television.

Jesseca White is claiming the Mad singer defamed her when he said she tried to trick him into believing he was the child's parent so she could get money out of him.

It came after she put him down as the father of her child on her birth certificate, but a later paternity test proved this was not the case.

According to TMZ, her lawsuit says Ne-Yo went onto VH1's Behind the Music show on September 2012 and said she was 'an unchaste woman who deliberately tricked him into believing he was the father of her child ... so that she could fraudulently bill him of 'ungodly amounts of money."'

She is suing for unspecified damages, and it remains to be seen whether the duo will come to a settlement agreement or battle it out in court.

The pair had been in a relationship when she fell pregnant, and following the child's birth in 2005 she named him as the father.

However, she insists she told the 33-year-old Las Vegas native at the time she was not sure he was the father.

Despite this he went ahead with his VH1  appearance, where he claimed she tried to aggressively defraud him.

Miss White claims that as a result of the show people have called her names, including 'c***,' 'b***h,' 'pathetic' and 'gold-digging b***h.'

She is also claiming that as a result of the interview her career as a photographer and a graphic designer is in tatters.

The couple had previously reached a settlement over the affair in 2009, in which Ne-Yo paid $575,000 to Jesseca, and in return she officially said the child was not his.

As part of the deal, both sides agreed to a confidentiality clause and promised never to go public about the incident.

This was of course before Ne-Yo's Behind the Music appearance, where he gave an account of his ordeal.

Last December, celebrity lawyer Gloria sent a letter to Ne-Yo accusing him of breaking the agreement, and claimed he falsely painted her client as a harlot who lied, broke his heart and then vanished.

She was seeking to have the matter settled out of court, but threatened to explore other legal options if this did not come to fruition.
