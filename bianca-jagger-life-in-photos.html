1

1945: Born in Nicaragua

Mondadori Portfolio//Getty Images

Born Blanca Pérez-Mora Macias on May 2, 1945, Bianca Jagger grew up in Managua, Nicaragua. Her father was an import-export businessman, whose success allowed her mother to stay at home with her and her two siblings.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

2

1955: Her Parents Divorce

Reg Lancaster//Getty Images

When Bianca was 10 years old, her parents filed for divorce. Her mother got custody of her and her siblings, but it was difficult to make ends meet as a single mother in Nicaragua during that time.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

3

1961: Moving to Paris

Art Zelin//Getty Images

At the age of 16, Bianca earned a full scholarship and enrolled in the Paris Institute of Political Studies. She grew increasingly interested in various political movements around the world, such as Mahatma Gandhi's nonviolent protests.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

4

1961: Becoming Bianca

Mike Lawn//Getty Images

After a year of living on her own abroad (and still only just 16 years old), she legally changed her name from Blanca to Bianca.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

5

1963: A Public Romance

Archive Photos//Getty Images

Bianca met British actor Michael Caine while studying in Paris. The pair were introduced through their tailor. They dated for a few years, and she even moved with him to England, but Bianca told People magazine in 1977, "Caine was unkind, superficial and kept me like I was his geisha."

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

6

1965: A Swift Breakup

REPORTERS ASSOCIES//Getty Images

At the age of 20, Bianca left Caine for French record mogul Eddie Barclay. She and Barclay dated for a few years, but according to People, she didn't want to marry him, because he was divorced: "I think marriage is important, and for him it was just a game."

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

7

1969: A Modeling Career

M. McKeown//Getty Images

Bianca established herself as a model in the late '60s and early '70s, and had acting ambitions as well. Here, she dons a black-and-white dress with an elaborate feathered hat for a photo shoot.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

8

1970: Meeting Mick Jagger

PA Images//Getty Images

When Bianca was 26 years old, she met Rolling Stones front man Mick Jagger. The couple were introduced at an after-party for a Rolling Stones concert in France. "It was like a bolt of lightning," she recalled to People.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

9

1970: A Whirlwind Courtship

Mirrorpix//Getty Images

After reportedly love at first sight, Mick convinced Bianca to join the band on tour in Italy, and thus their whirlwind romance began. Here, Bianca walks behind Mick and his bandmate Charlie Watts to a reception.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

10

1971: Getting Married

Reg Lancaster//Getty Images

After several months of dating, Mick proposed to his model girlfriend in 1971. The pair took off for the South of France, with Mick proposing just 24 hours before they walked down aisle.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

11

1971: The Wedding

Reg Lancaster//Getty Images

Bianca and Mick tied the knot on May 12, 1971, in an intimate Roman Catholic ceremony in St.-Tropez, France. The couple were famously flocked by paparazzi and fans outside of the church after word got out about the ceremony.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

12

1971: The Wedding Outfit

Express//Getty Images

The model wore an elegant YSL smoking jacket and wide-brimmed, veiled hat for her wedding. The ensemble instantly became an iconic bridal look and established Bianca as a force in fashion.

13

1971: The Honeymoon

Hulton Deutsch//Getty Images

The newlyweds set off for an Italian honeymoon after their nuptials. Bianca, who was four months pregnant at the time of their wedding, rides in a gondola with Mick in Venice. The couple also spent time aboard a yacht in Corsica and Sardinia.

14

1971: A Baby Girl

Larry Ellis//Getty Images

The couple welcomed a daughter together, Bianca's first and Mick's second, in 1971. Jade Sheena Jezebel Jagger was born on October 21 in Paris, France. Here, Jagger holds her daughter during a Rolling Stones concert in London.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

15

1972: Her On-Screen Debut

ullstein bild//Getty Images

The aspiring actress made her first on-screen appearance in the Rolling Stones documentary Cocksucker Blues.

16

1972: Going Back Home

Bettmann//Getty Images

Bianca returned home in 1972 after a devastating earthquake in her hometown of Managua, Nicaragua.

17

1973: Rumors of Affairs

Graham Wiltshire//Getty Images

Rock and roll's golden couple were known to have their fair share of indiscretions—on both sides of the union. In 1973, it was revealed that Bianca had an affair with actor Ryan O'Neal, while Mick was constantly surrounded by women and fangirls. "In the first years of our marriage, I played the wife. I was docile and subservient…but groupies weren't an easy thing to get used to," she told People in 1977. "I have had very few affairs. I could never have one without telling Mick," she said.

18

1973: Summers in the Hamptons

Central Press//Getty Images

Mick and Bianca were known to rent Andy Warhol's $5,000-a-month cottage in Montauk, where they would socialize with the '70s glamour crowd and escape the hot New York summer.

19

1973: Giving Back

PhotoQuest//Getty Images

Mick and Bianca meet with Ecuadorian statesman Galo Plaza after the Rolling Stones donated $350,000 from a relief concert to the country following a devastating earthquake.

20

1974: New York's It Girl

Francesco Scavullo//Getty Images

In 1974, Bianca graced the cover of Vogue, separating her career from her husband's. And in the mid-70's, the model began frequenting the nightclub scene.
