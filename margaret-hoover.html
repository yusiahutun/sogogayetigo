Table of Biography

Margaret Hoover is a conservative political pundit, political strategist, media personality, and author. Margaret Hoover is most known as the great-granddaughter of Herbert Hoover, the 31st President of the United States. Similarly, she is the anchor of the PBS revival of the conservative interview program Firing Line.

Early Life and Childhood

Margaret Hoover was born on the 11th of December in 1977 in Colorado, the United States. Margaret Claire Hoover is her given name. As of the year 2023, her age is 46 years old.

She is a citizen of the United States. She was born to Andrew Hoover, a father, and Jean Williams, a mother. Similarly, she is the great-granddaughter of Herbert Hoover, the 31st President of the United States.

Allan Hoover is her grandfather. She practices Christianity and her astrological sign is Sagittarius.

Education

In terms of schooling, she attended Graland Country Day School in Denver. She went to Bryn Mawr College after graduating from high school. In 2001, she earned a bachelor of arts degree in Spanish literature with a minor in political science from Bryn Mawr.

She also spent two years at Davidson College. She did, however, drop out of college before graduating.

Personal life

Margaret Hoover is a married lady. She is married to John Avlon. Avlon is a CNN contributor, former Rudy Giuliani speechwriter, senior columnist for Newsweek, and former Editor-in-Chief of The Daily Beast.

The couple has two children. Their first kid, a male named Jack, was born in 2013, and their second child, a daughter named Toula Lou, was born in 2015.

Professional Life

Margaret Hoover served for the George W. Bush administration as an Associate Director of Intergovernmental Affairs. She also assisted with George W. Bush’s reelection campaign. In 2006-07, she was the Deputy Finance Director for Rudy Giuliani’s presidential campaign.

She then worked as a Capitol Hill staffer for Congressman Mario Diaz-Balart. Similarly, she also served as an advisor to the Deputy Secretary of Homeland Security.

From 2008 through 2012, she was a Fox contributor. She was a guest on Bill O’Reilly’s show The O’Reilly Factor. She discusses the entertainment industry, Hollywood, popular culture, and politics.

Since 2012, she has been a CNN political contributor. She wrote the book American Individualism: How a New Generation of Conservatives Can Save the Republican Party. Furthermore, Crown Forum released the book in 2011.

In June 2018, she began presenting Firing Line on WNET.
