Nairobi Lady Sues Jimnah Mbaru for Refusing His Name to Be Added on Child's Birth Certificate

June 2024 · 3 minute read
  • A young woman is in court, seeking an order compelling businessman Jimnah Mbaru to pay child support
  • The 24-year-old woman claimed the wealthy businessman is the father of her child whom she birthed in 2018
  • In the suit papers, among other expenses, she is seeking KSh 320,000 monthly and 600,000 per year for school fees

A 24-year-old woman has moved to court to demand KSh 4.4 million annual child support from Kenyan businessman Jimnah Mbaru.

The lady claimed that Mbaru is the father of her child and opted to seek legal redress to compel him to provide child support over alleged negligence.

Her lawyer, Suyianka Lempaa, claimed the young mother got pregnant with the businessman in 2018.

She is seeking the said monies on grounds that the investor has neglected his parental responsibility for the said minor.

She alleged that Mbaru donated his parental responsibility to one Erick Murimi Kaburu through a power of attorney, treating the minor as a ‘property of chattel goods.’

It is her case that on or about September 1, 2020, the business Mogul gave power of attorney to the said Kaburu to be a "putative father" to the minor, terms of which are not known in any law she's aware of.

“In the said Memorandum of Understanding dated September 10, 2020, the "putative father" was required to pay KSh 50,000 per month, an amount which has not been remitted for the maintenance of the minor," reads her claims on court documents.

The woman's demands

In the suit papers, among other expenses, she is seeking KSh 320,000 monthly and 600,000 per year for school fees.

She wants the court to issue a maintenance order requiring Mbaru to pay for the minor's school fees and education-related expenses, medical cover, shelter, and food.

In the monthly expenses, she is seeking KSh 50,000 for food, KSh 50,000 for medical expenses, KSh 50,000 for clothes, KSh 100,000 for shopping, miscellaneous expenses KSh 20,000 and house help KSh 50,000.

She accused Mbaru of neglecting the minor by giving a stranger power of attorney to take care of the child's mental, physical, and emotional well-being, yet he is the biological father.

“Mbaru is a senior citizen, a prominent businessman who owns businesses worth billions of shillings including banking and control of Nairobi Security Exchange and therefore capable of supporting the child without any difficulties”, reads court documents.

Lady says Mbaru declined to have his name on birth certificate

She says Mbaru has refused to have his name indicated in the minor's birth certificate, therefore denying the child the benefit of a biological father.

She wants an order compelling Mbaru’s name to be included in the minor's birth certificate as his biological father.

The matter will be heard tomorrow.

Court grants man child custody despite in-law's protest

In a separate story, a Mombasa court granted a man his child physical custody despite his mother-in-law's protest over unpaid dowry.

The two were embroiled in a two-year legal battle over who should live with the child after the mother of the child's death.

Justice John Onyiego ruled in favour of the man despite the child's maternal grandmother wanting the custody, claiming that Kamba's culture dictates that a man who has not paid a dowry had no right of custody over the child or children arising.
