Russia-based footballer has 'family threatened'

May 2024 · 2 minute read

Russian-based winger Khvicha Kvaratskhelia has reportedly had his family threatened after refusing to leave Russian club Rubin Kazan.

The Georgian international’s relatives have reportedly been threatened due to the player continuing his career with the Russian Premier League side, where he has spent the past three seasons.

Kvaratskhelia has the option to leave Rubin Kazan, with FIFA allowing players in the Russian league to suspend their contracts with their clubs until summer 2022 due to Russia’s invasion of Ukraine. It is believed the Georgian had looked to stay put until his family were targeted, though the exact nature of the alleged threats remains unknown.

The 21-year-old midfielder has become one of Georgian football’s brightest talents after plying his trade in Russia. His current contract runs until 2024, with several big European clubs having been linked with a potential move this year.

Among those were West Ham and Leeds United, who reportedly considered bids in the region of £18million. And it now remains to be seen whether Kvaratskhelia will decide to follow other foreign players in leaving Russia following the attacks on Ukraine.

Kvaratskhelia is currently on international duty for the upcoming friendlies against Bosnia and Herzegovina and Albania. Questions have been raised as to whether he will report back for club duty after the Albania match on March 29.

The youngster has made 22 appearances across all competitions this season, notching two goals and five assists. He has already been capped 12 times by Georgia while scoring an impressive five goals along the way.
