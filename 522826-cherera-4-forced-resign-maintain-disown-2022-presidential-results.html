Cherera 4 Say They Were Forced to Resign, Maintain They Still Disown 2022 Presidential Results

August 2024 · 3 minute read
  • The "Cherera Four," former Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) commissioners, claimed they were forced to resign
  • T Irene Masit, Francis Wanderi, Juliana Cherera, and Justus Nyangaya appeared before the National Dialogue Team
  • Juliana Cherera, speaking virtually, explained that she had to leave the country due to threats and harassment, impacting her family and social life

Former Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) commissioners who were famously christened as Cherera Four have claimed they were forced to resign.

The Cherera Four, who were comprised of Irene Masit, Francis Wanderi, Juliana Cherera and Justus Nyangaya, appeared before the National Dialogue Team on Thursday, September 28 and revealed the intrigues behind their resignations.

Speaking before the committee, Wanderi disclosed that he and his counterparts were forced to relinquish their plum positions in the electoral commission.

"I want to admit that we were forced to resign. It was not our desire," he said.

Additionally, the former commissioners told the dialogue team that they did not get to lay their eyes on form 34C, adding they only saw the form that had a summary of the results from the devolved units.

What happened to Juliana Cherera?

On her part, Juliana Cherera, who joined the session virtually, said she had to escape abroad.

"I have a seven-year-old, and it was bad, even in school. Our social life was affected, which was bad for my children and family. We were threatened for what we saw and known," she said on the brink of tears.

She said their security was also withdrawn, and she decided to resign because of the controversy surrounding her and her counterparts.

"I decided that people would not go to the streets to demonstrate in my name. The next day, on a Monday morning, I resigned. I resigned for the sake of this country but I did not go to the commission to resign," she added.

On her part, Masit also said she escaped the country and used a motorbike to arrive at the airport for her flight.

Wafula Chebukati snubs dialogue by bipartisan team

Earlier, former chairman of the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC), Wafula Chebukati, turned down an invitation to appear before the National Dialogue Committee at Bomas.

In a statement, Chebukati stated that he had delivered his constitutional mandate and there was no reason to appear before the committee.

He explained that making oral presentations before the Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka and Majority Leader Kimani Ichung'wah-led team would betray his former staff.

In the statement signed by former commissioners Abdi Guliye and Boya Molu, Chebukati observed attending the session would amount to rewarding impunity.
