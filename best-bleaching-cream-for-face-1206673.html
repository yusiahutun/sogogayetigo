    How to Choose the Best Bleaching Cream for You?

    Bleaching creams are like boons with curses. They bring skin lightening and brightening solutions to those who want to freshen up their look, but over-bleaching of skin is a clear no-no. In light of the risks involved, a number of nations have outlawed the use of skin-bleaching products and ingredients involved. Below are a few pointers that will guide you on how to choose the best bleaching creams without causing harm to your skin.

    a) Ingredients: A bleaching cream or creme may comprise a number of different chemical agents and compounds. A few of them work better than any others. They may indeed be perfectly harmless yet completely ineffectual, or the compounds could cause negative effects and be toxic. These might be generally benign (for example, lemon juice), poisonous (camphor), unpleasant (detergents), or allergenic (hair dye). 

    b) Side effects: Skin-bleaching products may lead to contact dermatitis and/or skin redness, blisters, skin ulcers, hives, dry scaly skin, swelling, itching, or burning sensation.

    Pro Tip

    Always do a patch test before directly applying it to your face. This prevents face burns, bumps, inflammation, rashes, and allergies.

    c) Benefits: You should be clear about the purpose and effectiveness of using a bleaching cream. While these can help reduce the appearance of dark spots, age spots, melasma, freckles, eczema, psoriasis marks, and acne scars, they cannot heal acne or treat any sort of inflammation or irritation. Bleaching creams may help balance out your skin tone by reducing hyperpigmented spots and sun damage.
