Chalkboard Fridges Are Having a Moment

October 2024 · 2 minute read

If you're ready to upgrade your family's most-used appliance, but don't want to drop a bucket of cash on a new fridge, this cheap and easy DIY solution may be the answer.

For homeowners who no longer love the look or color of their fridges, but don't want to chuck a perfectly good appliance, consider dousing it with a coat of chalkboard paint. This family-friendly idea will instantly give your appliance new life and make it more functional.

[pinterest ]https://www.pinterest.com/pin/144255994291565005/[/pinterest]

This quick craft can be completed in an afternoon with about $25 worth of supplies. You can customize the look by choosing a shade of chalkboard paint that fits with your kitchen's style: Green will evoke a vintage-y vibe that will look beautiful next to rich woods and antique pieces, while black looks more modern and will contrast beautifully with white subway tile or cabinetry.

In addition to disguising a fridge's outdated finish, chalkboard paint means you can jot down your shopping list, weekly meal plan, recipes, and food diary all in one spot. "A chalkboard fridge is exactly what it sounds like, but it's also so much more than that," Faith Durand wrote for The Kitchn. "A chalkboard fridge becomes a culinary journal, helping you to reflect on what kind of an eater you are." Plus, it's fun for kiddos too. Parents and grandparents can keep little ones occupied with some colorful sidewalk chalk while they prep dinner.

Upkeep is easy, too. You can simply wipe it down with a wet cloth when you're ready for a clean slate, and the paint should last for several years, even on textured fridges. "Our fridge hasn't chipped once," Ashley wrote on her blog The Handmade Home. "It's held up great, and we're so happy we changed it over. I think the key to that was the primer."

Get an easy tutorial at The Kurtz Corner, and stock up on these supplies before you get started.

Don't want to paint? Decals are also a fun, mess-free way to achieve a similar look!
