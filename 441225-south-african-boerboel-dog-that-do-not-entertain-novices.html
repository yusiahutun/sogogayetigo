Boerboel: All You Need to Know About South African Dog Breed That Doesn't Entertain Novices

February 2024 Â· 3 minute read
  • Those looking for dogs to domesticate always go for the ones with appealing muscularity and body physique
  • The well-bodied canines are believed to be protective and confident enough to face any intruder causing trouble
  • South African Boerboel is believed to fit the bill, thanks to its speed, overly developed muscles and appropriate temperament

A family lost its kin in Malindi, Kilifi county, after her employer's dog attacked her as she carried on with her daily routine.

The Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) informed the public that 39-year-old Roselyn Nafuna succumbed to injuries she sustained following the attack.

The agency further specified that the killer dog was a Boerboel, a renowned breed from South Africa, famed for its muscularity and no-nonsense nature when it encounters a perceived enemy.

TUKO.co.ke explored some of the canine's facts that anyone dealing with or coming across it ought to be cognisant of.

1. Origin

Also known as "mastiff", Boerboel is a South African breed.

Its name was derived from two Afrikaans terms; Boer, a farmer, and boel, a dog.

In essence, Boerboel is bred by farmers for domestication purposes, thus the name "farmers dog".,

2. Temperament

When not provoked, Boerboel is an obedient and loyal canine.

However, whenever its space is invaded, it proves how confident and protective it is.

Boerboel is known to be intolerant should it find itself in the midst of other breeds.

It is protective of its young ones and the humans with whom it cohabits.

American Kennel Club recommends that a Boerboel's training and socialisation should begin early while it is still in the pup stages.

The dog is a trainable and versatile breed, eager to spend time with its adored humans.

3. Body Attributes

As stated earlier stated, Boerboel is well-bodied with well-developed muscles, presumably why many prefer it.

It breeds in different colours, including Black, Red, Brown, Brindle, Cream and Fawn.

Boerboel's life span ranges between 10 and 12 years.

A mature female dog averagely weighs between 55kg and 73kg, with a male one weighing between 60kg and 79kg.

A typical Boerboel is characterised by a black muzzle and a short coat, not forgetting a blocky head.

4. Market Price

An adult Boerboel dog is valued at not less than R10,000 (equivalent to KSh 75,000) in South Africa, its country of origin.

In Kenya, the price ranges between KSh 10,000 and KSh 120,000 depending on size, age and the cost of upbringing.
