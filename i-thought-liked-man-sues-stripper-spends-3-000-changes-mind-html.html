A man who spent nearly $3,000 on a stripper is suing her to get the money back, claiming that it was a loan.

However, the stripper says that ‘she doesn’t believe in loans’.

Robert Wallace, 32, from Houston, is bringing the suit against a dancer with the stage name of Nomi.

Lawsuit: Robert Wallace (pictured) is suing a stripper after she failed to return $2,000, a laptop and DVDs

'I don't believe in loans': Nomi said that she wouldn't have accepted cash and various items from Mr Wallace if they weren't gifts

 

He met her a year ago at a nightclub called Treasures in Houston and claims to have spent the money on her when they began a relationship.

Mr Wallace says that he lent her around $2,000 along with a laptop and some Harry Potter DVDs.

He told Fox 26: ‘We had a dating relationship and on May 3 we had broken up. I know how stubborn she can be, so after waiting for a week for her to return my things, I decided to file a suit to get my stuff back.’

Counter-claim: Nomi said she'd given Mr Wallace gifts, too - her 'booty and boobs'

However, Nomi claims that the relationship was platonic and that she had no idea that the items and money named in the suit weren’t gifts.

She said: ‘I’ve given him gifts, too. How do I get my booty and boobs back.’

She added that she doesn’t believe in loans because she doesn’t want to have to pay anything back.

A court date has yet to be set.
