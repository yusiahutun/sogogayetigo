10 best books by African authors you should read right now

February 2024 · 7 minute read

If you seek to learn more about African literature, then you should consider reading the best books by African authors. Most of the authors have the books that will keep you turning the pages all night. Some of these remarkable authors include Chinua Achebe, Elechi Amadi, Ngugi wa Thiong’o, and Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie.

Some African authors have gone overboard with their creativity over the years, publishing the best books ever. Their work inspires many people across the continent. Most of their books focus on true life events, while others detail the pre-colonial and post-colonial events, and romance.

10 best books by African authors

Are you a fan of African literature books? There are many best-selling and must-read books by African writers you might find worthwhile to read. Some of them include:

1. Things Fall Apart by Chinua Achebe

Things Fall Apart is one of the best novels to have ever been authored by the late Chinua Achebe, a Nigerian professor, novelist, critic, and poet. The novel was originally published in 1958 by Heinemann publishers.

The story follows through the life of Okonkwo, who is the main character. He is a respected leader, warrior, farmer, and hypermasculine individual. He lives through generations; the pre-colonial and colonial eras in Nigeria. He lives an opposite life to that of his late father, who is considered a lazy and coward man.

Overall, major themes in the novel are colonialism and its diverse impacts, social transformation and tradition, masculinity, tribal beliefs/customs, sense of justice, and social disintegration.

2. The Concubine by Elechi Amadi

The Concubine by Elechi Amadi is another great novel by a Nigerian author. Heinemann African Writers Series published the 216-page novel in 1966. So far, it remains to be one of the best African fiction books the author wrote before he died in 2016.

The novel tells the story of a young beautiful widow by the name Ihuoma. She lives in a village guided by traditions. After the death of her husband, Ihuoma becomes the dream of every young man in the village. She eventually falls in love with a hard-working hunter by the name Ekwueme.

The story-telling skills employed by the author makes the novel worth reading. The themes of the book are another aspect to consider. Get a copy of the book to learn more about the incredible story of Ihuoma and Ekwueme.

3. Born a Crime by Trevor Noah

Born a Crime is a debut novel by Trevor Noah, a South African comedian. The autographical comedy book was originally published by Spiegel & Grau in November 2016. It contains 304pages. As of 2021, the book has won three awards; NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Literary Work - Debut Author and Biography / Auto-biography (2017) and Audie Award for Best Male Narrator (2018).

The book covers the comedian's stories while growing in South Africa at the end of the Apartheid era. Noah was born by a South African mother and a Swiss-German father. During the time, it was forbidden. So, most of the time, he was hidden by his mother to protect him from society.

The book's major themes are racism, apartheid, identity, the cycle of poverty, love and personal growth, resilience through religion, education, and humour.

4. A Grain of Wheat by Ngugi wa Thiong’o

A Grain of Wheat by Ngugi wa Thiong’o is another great novel by the established Kenyan author. The book was originally published by Heinemann African Writers Series in 1967. The 247 pages novel is among the good African books you do not want to miss reading.

The novel tells a story of Mugo and his village people, who live during the Mau Mau rebellion period (1952–1960). He is the Moses of his generation. Other characters in the novel are Karanja, Gikonyo, and Mumbi. Some of the novel's main themes are the struggle for independence and its aftermath, moral/social issues, guilt and redemption, and racism.

5. Season of Crimson Blossoms by Abubakar Adam Ibrahim

Season of Crimson Blossoms by Abubakar Adam Ibrahim is an adult fiction and romantic fantasy novel. Parrésia Publishers originally published the 346-page novel in 2015. A year later, it won the Nigeria Prize for Literature award.

Season of Crimson Blossoms documents the story of Hassan Reza (25-year-old gang and drug dealer) and Binta Zubairu (55-year-old widow and grandmother). The two are involved in an illicit affair. It is a story of love that defies age, religion, and class. The story is set in the outskirts of Abuja, Nigeria.

6. Half of a Yellow Sun by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

Half of a Yellow Sun is a captivating novel by award-winning Nigerian novelist, Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie. It was originally published by Knopf/Anchor in 2006. It is the top choice if you are looking for the best African literature books on politics.

Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie focuses on the story of the Biafran War. Nigeria, with the help of Russia and Britain, fight the Biafrans. The main characters are Olana, Ugwu, Odenigbo, Richard, and Kainene. The novel's themes are ethnic tension, love and betrayal, construction of nationhood, and postcolonial trauma.

7. Broken Glass by Alain Mabanckou

Alain Mabanckou’s Broken Glass is a fictional novel. It was originally published by Soft Skull Press in 2005. It is a short novel containing 176 pages.

The author focuses on the life of a former school teacher, nicknamed Broken Glass. He is appointed to document stories of his society’s posterity. However, he fails despondently because he is always in trouble. The story is set in Congo. Overall, the novel is a perfect example of a mocking satire of the risks of artistic integrity.

8. Arrow of God by Chinua Achebe

Arrow of God is another novel authored by Chinua Achebe, who is among the best African authors to have ever lived. The 287 pages novel was originally published by Heinemann in 1964. Similar to most of his other works, Arrow of God is a fictional novel.

Achebe tells the story of two communities, Umuaro and Okperi, during the 1920s. The two communities clash over a piece of disputed land. The main character, Ezeulu – chief priest of the god Ulu, confronts Christian missionaries and colonialists on behalf of his people to retain his authority within the two communities. Unfortunately, he leads his people to destruction.

9. Homegoing by Yaa Gyasi

If you are looking for books on African culture, you need to consider Homegoing by Yaa Gyasi. The novel was originally published in June 2016. The events of the story are set during the mid-1700s when Ghana was known as the Gold Coast.

The novel is centred on the stories of two half-sisters; Effie and Esi. Effi is married to an Englishman while Esie is sold as a slave to America. Her children and grandchildren are brought up in foreign land.

The chapters of the novel feature the lives of Effi’s and Esi’s children in Ghana and the US, respectively. The novel's themes are family legacy, slavery, history, gender stereotypes, sexism, and violence.

10. Americanah by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

Americanah by Chimamanda Ngozi is, undeniably, among the captivating books written by African authors. The novel was published by Alfred A. Knopf publishers in 2013. It contains 608 pages.

It is a powerful story about Ifemelu and Obinze. The two young lovers are forced to leave their homeland to political instability. Ifemelu departs for the US while Obinze heads to London. Contrary to her expectation, Ifemelu discovers the American dream is a lie.

Her stay in the US, as a black person, becomes unbearable because of how she is treated. Fifteen years later, they return to their country, finding it more favourable than the Western countries.
