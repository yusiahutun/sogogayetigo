Tyler Cameron Biography TimeLine

Tyler Cameron is a popular American television personality and model, best known for his appearance on the fifteenth season of the reality show “The Bachelorette.” Born on January 31, 1993, in Jupiter, Florida, Cameron had a successful college football career before transitioning to modeling and reality television. Despite the loss of both his parents at a young age, he has persevered and continues to pursue his passions. His charity work, modeling career, and television appearances have made him a beloved figure among fans of “The Bachelorette” and beyond.

  • 1993: Tyler Cameron is born in Jupiter, Florida.
  • 2011-2015: Cameron attends Wake Forest University and plays football as a quarterback for the Demon Deacons.
  • 2016: Cameron graduates from Wake Forest University with a degree in Communication.
  • 2017: Tyler’s mother, Andrea Cameron, dies of a brain aneurysm at age 55. He establishes the nonprofit, The Andrea Cameron Foundation, to raise funds for the prevention of, and research into, the condition.
  • 2018: Tyler begins his modeling career and is signed with Next Model Management in Miami, Florida.
  • 2019: Tyler becomes a contestant on the fifteenth season of “The Bachelorette,” starring Hannah Brown. He becomes a fan favorite and ultimately finishes as the runner-up.
  • 2019: After “The Bachelorette,” Tyler briefly dates supermodel Gigi Hadid.
  • 2020: Tyler stars in the reality show “Barkitecture” with fellow “The Bachelorette” contestant, Matt James.
  • 2020: Tyler’s father, Jeff Cameron, dies of a heart attack at age 55.
  • 2021: Tyler launches a line of sunglasses called “Cameron Collection” in partnership with Australian eyewear company, Quay.
  • 2021: Tyler appears as a guest on the reality show “The Bachelor” and begins hosting a podcast called “Bachelors in the City” with fellow “The Bachelorette” contestant, Matteo Valles.
  • 2022: Tyler becomes a co-host of the American version of “Love Island” alongside Arielle Vandenberg.
