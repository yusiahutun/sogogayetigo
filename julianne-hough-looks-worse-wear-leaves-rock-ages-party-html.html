She arrived on the red carpet looking sexy, fresh and bright-eyed and ready for a fun night at the Rock of Ages London premiere.

But just a few hours later it was a different story for Julianne Hough as she left the after-party looking rather worse for wear.

The dancer appeared bedraggled and unsteady on her feet as she left the bash which was held at the Roka nightclub.

Rock (Un)Steady! Julianne Hough looked a little worse for wear as she was helped to her car after the Rock of Ages post-premiere party in London last night

She gripped onto a member of the security team as he led her out to a waiting car.

And amazingly, Julianne, 23, managed to maintain her dignity in her extremely plunging dress, and thankfully avoided a wardrobe mishap that would have left her feeling very red-faced.

Which way is home? Julianne looked happy to bed led away from the party

She was joined at the party by her co-stars, singer Mary J Blige and Malin Akerman, who looked a lot brighter than Hough.

But Akerman, as fresh-faced as she appeared, still struggled to protect her modesty as she got in her car and tugged her dress over her knees.

Rock chicks: Mary J Blige and Malin Akerman, who star alongside Hough in the film, were also spotted leaving the party in better shape than her

Julianne plays waitress and aspiring singer Sherrie Christian in the movie, who ends up working in a strip club when she falls on hard times.

However, things don’t quite work out straight away and she spends time working in the ‘gentleman’s club’ and has a one-night stand with rocker Stacee Jaxx - played by Tom Cruise - before finding true love.

Easy does it! Malin eased herself into the back of a car as she desperately tried to spare her blushes

She was at the premiere in London last night and she was clearly channelling both sides of her character last night in a black dress with cute white puffball sleeves which added an air of innocence to the outfit, while the daringly low V-neck showed off her sexy side. 

And she must have been using some special double sided tape to keep everything in place as she walked the red carpet in her low cut dress.

Sexy and sweet! Julianne Hough covers all bases in her low cut dress with cute puffball sleeves at the Rock of Ages premiere in London

She paired the dress with high-heeled, black peep-toe shoes.

A beehive hairstyle, held back with a thin black band completed the look.

Based on the Broadway musical of the same name, Rock of Ages tells the tale of Sherrie Christian and a busboy who fall in love during the Eighties glam metal era.

Low cut: Julianne grabs attention with her low cut dress in London tonight

Julianne’s character, a native of the US Midwest travels to LA in pursuit of her Hollywood dream to be a singer. 

Jaxx is played by Hollywood star Tom Cruise and Julianne – who is dating American Idol host  Ryan Seacrest - admitted working with the heartthrob made her freak out.

Julianne posed with her castmates at the European premiere of Rock of Ages in London tonight

She told flicksandbits, ‘The rock star in Tom came out, he is a rock star in this, it was incredible to just watch him. 

‘Every time I see Pour Some Sugar On Me, I freak out like a little girl.

‘I actually am kind of a little girl, I have them butterflies, I’m thinking, “That is like the sexiest man I’ve ever seen in my life on stage singing Pour Some Sugar On Me”….it doesn’t get better than that!’ 

She also revealed that one scene even called for her to give Tom a lapdance.

Julianne stars as small-town girl Sherrie who dreams of finding fame in the new movie

And despite the scene being cut from the version in theatres, Julianne admitted that particular day on set has been emblazoned in her memory. 

Speaking on Live! With Kelly she said: 'It got caught because apparently mothers didn't like Sherrie Christian after that. It was a little too hot and steamy and sexy. It will be on the director's cut, though.'

She added: 'Let’s just say I’ll always have that in my back pocket, that I gave Tom Cruise a lap dance.'

Julianne was thrilled to be in London for the premiere, tweeting it felt like being home
