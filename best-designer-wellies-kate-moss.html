The 8 best pairs of designer wellington boots to shop this festival season

August 2024 · 3 minute read

These are the chicest festival wellies to don in 2024...

    Once reserved for fishermen and dog walkers, the most unexpected noughties footwear revival has to be that of the humble welly.

    No image of wellington boots is more etched in our memory than that of Kate Moss walking arm in arm with Pete Doherty at Glastonbury in 2005, bare legs out and dark navy Hunter wellies on.

    The look became so iconic that it reportedly saved Hunter from going into administration, thanks to a dramatic sales increase. Today the British heritage brand continues to enjoy the Moss-effect and is popular for festival goers and farmers alike. 

    Fast forward to 2024, the waterproof footwear saviour is a total festival must-have - especially where British weather is concerned...

    How we chose:

    • Style and festival-appropriate: Naturally, style is paramount. All the pairs of wellington boots we've highlighted below are chic, durable, and will protect your feet against any unfavourable festival weather. 
    • Brand: The picks we've highlighted below are all by designer labels, ideal if you're looking to treat yourself to a slice of festival luxury.

    Hello! Fashion shares the best designer wellies to shop now:

    • Best Designer Wellies

      Glittered Chain-Embellished Rubber Rain Boots

      JW Anderson

      Talk about all that glitters... These gold wellies by JW Anderson are guaranteed to steal the show, irrespective of the festival headliner. If the mud situation isn't too severe, the shorter length can work really well and the oversized chain detailing adds extra interest. 

      £290.00 £189.00 AT THE OUTNET 

    • Diorunion Rain Boot

      Crafted in Italy, these boots by Dior feel very much aligned with the heritage label's luxe, timeless aesthetic. The colour palette is unfussy and versatile, and showcases the 'L'Union Fait la Force' motto, arranged with stars - Christian Dior's lucky symbol. 

      £990.00 AT DIOR 

    • Women's Double G Rain Boot

      Cut from an earthy khaki rubber and made in Italy, these wellington boots by Gucci are sleek and minimalist, while also paying soft tribute to the house's historic emblem on the outer sides. There is a little bit of height on the heel for a subtle boost. 

      £615.00 AT GUCCI 

    • Wellington Boots

      Kenzo x Hunter

      Iconic British welly manufacturer Hunter has teamed up with exuberant French fashion label Kenzo, and the result is really playful. The design combines Hunter's classic silhouette with Kenzo's signature Boke Flower and these would look great alongside a glossy PVC coat. 

      £205.00 AT FARFETCH 

    • Leopard-Trim Wellington Boots

      Roberto Cavalli

      Black gloss and leopard print are a winning combination, and this pair by Roberto Cavalli are testament to that. These would look great with a fur coat and dark shades if you're determined to take the mob wife aesthetic to a whole new level.

      £276.00 £166.00 AT FARFETCH 

    • Betty Ankle Rain Boot

      Chloé's version of wellington boots are giving cold-weather luxe. Cut from beige rubber, they feature elegant shearling trims and I love the line of the heightened heel for glamorous outdoor energy.

      £550.00 AT CHLOÉ

    • Logo Print Rain Boots

      Patou x Le Chameau

      French heritage footwear label Le Chameau has teamed up with cool-girl label Patou on this pair of cute lavender wellies, hence the double logo design. The monochrome stripe texture detailing on the uppers adds interest to an otherwise quite understated (bar the lively shade) boot.

      £395.00 £316.00 AT FARFETCH 

    • High Celine Rain Boots

      Biker obsessives will adore this sludgy green pair by Celine. Featuring edgy hardware, they nod to one of the biggest boot trends of the moment, while remaining true to the French luxury label's roots, via the Triomphe emblem.

      £890.00 AT CELINE
