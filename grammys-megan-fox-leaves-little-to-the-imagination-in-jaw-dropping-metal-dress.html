Grammys: Megan Fox leaves little to the imagination in jaw-dropping metal dress

April 2024 · 3 minute read

Megan Fox and Machine Gun Kelly were noticeably missing from the 2024 Grammy Awards, but fear not, we still got a killer outfit from the Transformers star.

The Hollywood star stepped out for the 2024 Jam for Janie Grammy Awards Viewing Party and left little to the imagination with her ‘barely-there’ see-through dress. But it wasn’t just Megan Fox‘s outfit that stole the limelight, she also debuted yet another new hairstyle, and we’re obsessed. Machine Gun Kelly, however, was nowhere to be seen.

Megan Fox debuts Marshmallow pink hair in ‘barely there’ dress

Although she didn’t actually attend the Grammy Awards ceremony, the 37-year-old definitely stole the show.

Stepping out on the Red Carpet for the viewing party, the star debuted a lighter Marshmallow pink hair, just months after stepping out with fiery red locks.

Hair stylist Dimitris Giannetos showed off the look on his Instagram page, as one follower asked: “Is this her real hair or a wig?” to which he responded: “Not a wig.”

Now onto the dress. The movie star sent jaws dropping, going topless underneath her skimpy chain metal dress. She wore a fur coat over the dress but removed it for the cameras so we could see the fashion dream in all its glory, which was paired with silver chrome stilettos. Topping off the rockstar look, the mom of three sported a lip charm as she posed for the cameras, showing off her sleeve of tattoos.

She posted the picture to Instagram, with many fans saying she looked ‘unrecognizable’ and ‘MGK coded.’

“Don’t get me wrong, these pictures are stunning. However, that’s not Megan Fox anymore. It doesn’t look anything like her,” wrote one.

“This is so MGK coded, the new sleeve, the pink hair, the diamond chainmail, the lip jewelry….,” another commented.

A third wrote: “One thing about Megan Fox… she knows how to make a comeback.” Yes she does!

MGK says an award ‘doesn’t mean anything’

Although he wasn’t in attendance at the award ceremony, the Bloody Valentine singer attended Clive Davis’ Pre-Grammy Gala, where he said an award “doesn’t mean anything.”

Asked what it would mean to win a Grammy one day, MGK, real name Colson Baker told USA Today: “I hope to be at a place mentally where I’m just proud of myself no matter what. Like an award doesn’t mean anything.”

When the reported asked him if he ‘was there’ yet, the 33-year-old replied: “No. I’ll get there. I would like a Grammy.”

The singer received a nomination for Best Rock Album at the 2023 Grammy Awards for Mainstream Sellout but was beaten by Ozzy Osbourne‘s Patient Number 9.
