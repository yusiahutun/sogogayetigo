Where was Jermaine Jackson? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

On December 11, 1954, Katherine Jackson and Joseph Jackson welcomed Jermaine La Jaune Jackson into the world in Gary, Indiana. His siblings include Janet, Randy, Michael, Marlon, La Toya, Tito, Jackie, and Rebbie.

In 1973, he received his diploma from Van Nuys, Los Angeles’ Birmingham High School. He and his siblings began playing their father’s guitar at a young age.

Is Jermaine Jackson Married? Relationship

In 1973, Jermaine Jackson wed Hazel Gordy. Their three children are Jaimy Jackson, Autumn Joy Jackson, and Jermaine La Jaune “Jay” Jackson Jr. The pair were divorced in 1988. During the years 1986 to 1993, he shared a home with Margaret Maldonado. Jeremy Maldonado and Jourdynn Michael were their two sons.

In 1995, he wed Alejandra Oaziaza. Jermajesty and Jaafar, their two children, are his and hers. They got a divorce in 2003. He wed Halima Rashid in 2004.

How did Jermaine Jackson start his Professional Career?

As a member of “The Jackson Five,” Jermaine Jackson issued the single “Big Boy” in 1967. Between 1968 and 1969, the trio, who had a contract with Motown Records, issued several singles. He was a co-lead singer for the group. Their most recent album, which they worked on together, was released in 1989.

He issued his debut solo album, named “Jermaine,” in 1972. In the United States, it topped the R&B charts. A year later, he released the album “Come Into My Life.” He issued “My Name Is Jermaine” in 1976.

He issued the album “Feel the Fire” in 1977. Jackson’s previous albums include “Frontiers,” “Let’s Get Serious,” “Jermaine,” “I Like Your Style,” “Let Me Tickle Your Fancy,” “Jermaine Jackson,” “Precious Moments,” “Don’t Take It Personally,” “You Said,” and “I Wish You L.O.V.E.”

Additionally, he has seven compilation albums, including “Ultimate Collection” and “Heritage Collection,” available. He is best known for his hit singles “Daddy’s Home,” “Let’s Get Serious,” “Let Me Tickle Your Fancy,” “Do What You Do,” “When the Rain Begins to Fall,” and “Don’t Take It Personal.”

Awards and Achievements of Jermaine Jackson

For “The Jacksons: An American Dream,” Jermaine Jackson was nominated in 1993 for an Emmy Award for Outstanding Miniseries. Five Grammy Awards in various categories have nominated him. He and the Jackson 5 were honored with induction into the Vocal Group Hall of Fame in 1999. In 1997, they were admitted to the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
