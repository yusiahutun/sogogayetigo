The Connection of Journaling with Mental Health

June 2024 · 7 minute read

To better comprehend your thoughts and feelings, put them in writing. Additionally, journaling might be a fantastic option if you deal with stress, despair, or anxiety. Writing down your feelings and ideas as you go through daily life is called journaling. Especially when you’re depressed or nervous, journaling can help you process and understand your feelings.

You can develop, become more self-aware, and obtain insightful knowledge with its assistance. A diary is memorabilia that is written or recorded. It is organized chronologically and contains discrete entries that detail events that occur during a given day or other time. Although handwritten diaries have always been the norm, they are now frequently digital. It’s acceptable to start journaling with a brief entry as you get started. Don’t force yourself to write on a certain topic or in a certain quantity. Simply start by writing down any ideas or emotions that come to you. You can gradually advance to writing lengthier entries or delving further into particular subjects.

It’s a very private encounter that can happen in a variety of ways.

You never know when journaling will resemble an old-fashioned diary entry from your adolescent years. It could be a list of things that make you happy or a list of objectives you wish to accomplish the next day.

Types Of Journaling

1. Daily Journaling:

This is a journal that you write in every day, as the name would imply. But unlike other forms of journaling, the contents are different since each day you share what you did and how you feel about it. Those who are going through life transitions or who want to record a certain time in their lives may find this kind of journaling useful. It might also be a good place to start when embarking on a new professional endeavour. Maintaining a daily notebook will be a valuable tool for reviewing your progress over time. It can also be used as a guide if you think life is passing you by too rapidly.

2. Using Visual Journals:

The majority of individuals imagine writing when they consider beginning a notebook. However, visual journaling consists primarily of pictures. Every entry tells your narrative with illustrations. Simple line drawings, storyboards, comic strips, or stylized sketches can all be used for these. Try out various drawing styles to determine which one suits you the best.

For those who find it difficult to express themselves verbally or who do not enjoy writing, this kind of journalism is beneficial. You may feel that writing in a journal is too limited and would rather see your entries displayed more graphically.

3. Free Writing:

Free writing is a common writing warm-up before beginning a novel or other lengthy work. However, it can also be a helpful tool for beginning a journal. Writing in a journal as your ideas come to you is known as stream-of-consciousness journaling.

Keep a Gratitude Diary

Research indicates that happiness and thankfulness are related. It has been demonstrated that cultivating appreciation and a strong practice of thankfulness improves resilience in people as well as interpersonal connections. Thus, beginning to keep a thankfulness diary can be very advantageous. There are advantages to even adding a few bullets to your current journaling practice listing the things or people you are grateful for.

Using Bullets Journal

Perhaps you’ve seen and wondered how to utilize a bullet journal. They direct your entries with evenly spaced dots rather than lines. One can customize bullet journals to a great extent. You can use them to monitor your daily steps and your mood. Alternatively, you may use a single page as an agenda that has bullets for reflections like “my intention for today” or “one thing that made my day today.” Additionally, you may express your creativity by using various colours and mediums to customize the appearance of your diary entries.

Perks of Keeping a Journal:

Even though writing things down seems like a simple enough task, the benefits are significant. These are but a few advantages of journaling.

1. Enhances Mental Health:

Our lives are now marked by an unparalleled level of tension and uncertainty because of the COVID-19 epidemic. Four out of ten US individuals report having had symptoms of anxiety or sadness during this time. Using a notebook as a healthy outlet for strong feelings and uncertainties is one strategy to manage them during trying times. It has been demonstrated that journaling improves mental health and lessens the impacts of despair and anxiety.

2. Increases Healing time and Immune System Strength:

Probably everyone has heard the saying, “An apple a day keeps the doctor away.” It turns out that journaling can accomplish the same goal. Writing in a journal not only promotes quicker healing but also has a long-term positive impact on health outcomes. According to a different study, writing expressively aided older persons’ wound recovery.

3. Provide a Space for you to show your Gratitude:

Keeping a gratitude diary and listing your blessings is one of the best methods to show your thanks. It has been demonstrated that gratitude activates brain regions linked to happy feelings. Additionally, gratitude helps you overcome bad feelings, increase optimism, and deepen your compassion.

4. Assists you in Overcoming Obstacles

It has been demonstrated that journaling aids in the healing of past traumas and difficult events. Participants in a recent Duke University study who had just gone through a traumatic event were asked to participate in a six-week writing “intervention.” This included a range of writing exercises, including transactional, poetic, expressive, and mindful journaling. Writing improved participants’ resilience and reduced stress, according to the study.

5. Assists you in Setting and Achieving goals

Writing down your objectives is one of the best strategies to accomplish them. Writing down your objectives makes them easier for you to see. CEOs and professional sportsmen alike use visualization as a potent strategy. It entails seeing your desired outcome as already within reach. 2020 saw the discovery by Dr. Gail Matthews of the Dominican University of California that those who put their goals in writing are more likely to succeed than those who don’t.

Summing up

Being consistent is the only way to enjoy all the benefits that journaling has to offer. This entails turning journaling from an infrequent pastime to a routine. A potent method for doing inner work is to write every day. It can facilitate the processing of challenging feelings and circumstances and lead to breakthroughs and insights. This is a subjective choice that may evolve. To help you get clear on the kind of profession you desire, you may start a notebook and then modify it to incorporate a goal plan. Writing in a notebook is an excellent way to practice mindfulness because it keeps your attention in the here and now. It is a serene sensation to be in the current moment and not worry about the past or the future. Although the procedure is straightforward, it can be intimidating to stare at the first blank page in your notebook.
