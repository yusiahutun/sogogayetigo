Robert Mugabe: Family late Zimbabwe president left behind

April 2024 Â· 3 minute read

The world is still coming to terms with the passing of former Zimbabwean leader, Robert Mugabe, and TUKO.co.ke decided to take a look at the people he has left behind.

Here is a look into the lives of the people he has left behind - his wife and three children.

Grace Mugabe

Grace Mugabe is the second wife of the late former Zimbabwean president and the two tied the knot in 1996.

Grace was born on July 23 in Benoni, South Africa, to migrant parents. She is the fourth of five children. Her father stayed in South Africa to work and support his family - which had since moved to Rhodesia.

The late African dictator was not Grace's first husband - a 19-year-old Grace had been married to an air force pilot, Stanley Goreraza, with whom she had a son, Russell Goreraza.

At the time of their wedding, Grace was 31 and Mugabe was 72-years old, according to Wikipedia.

Two years after tying the knot, Grace gave birth to their third child, Chatunga Berlamine Mugabe.

Michael Nhamodzenyika Mugabe (deceased)

Mugabe did not leave Michael Nhamodzenyika Mugabe behind when he died, but we thought it was important to name his first child in the article.

Mugabe and his first wife, Sally Hayfron, had Michael on 27 September, 1966.

However, at the age of just three, Michael passed away in Ghana due to malaria, The South African reported.

Bona Mugabe

Bona is Mugabe's only daughter and the first child he had with Grace. She was named after the former Zimbabwean president's mother and is currently 31-years old.

She has a child of her own with her husband, Simba Chikore, who was appointed as chief operating officer at Air Zimbabwe.

Their 2014 wedding at Mugabe's Harare mansion cost a reported $4.49 million - which is an estimated KSh 466,196,700.

Robert Mugabe Jr.

His eldest living son, Robert Mugabe Jr, is reportedly a fashion designer.

According to a report by News 24 in 2018, Mugabe Jr said he drew inspiration for his designs from his mother.

Chatunga Bellarmine Mugabe

Chatunga Bellarmine Mugabe was born in 1997. He has been criticised by Zimbabweans for his partying lifestyle in South Africa.

The 22-year-old reportedly owned a butchery business, which News24 reported he was at risk of losing in 2018.
