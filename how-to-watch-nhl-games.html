The 2023-2024 NHL regular season has begun, which will have any hockey fan excited to watch their favorite team on the ice. But if you don’t have a cable subscription (or want a cheaper option), you may be wondering how to watch NHL games live for free. Don’t sweat it, because we did the research and these are the best services to stream and save with this season.

The National Hockey League, also known as the NHL, was founded in 1917 in Montreal, Quebec, Canada. The league consists of 32 teams total (24 in the United States and 7 in Canada). There are two conferences: the Eastern Conference and the Western Conference. Within the Eastern Conference, there’s the Atlantic Division and Metropolitan Division. Within the Western Conference, there’s the Central Division and Pacific Division. In the 2023 NHL regular season, which usually takes place from early October through early April, each team plays 82 games. From April through June, 16 teams compete in the Stanley Cup playoffs. The final two teams face off in the Stanley Cup Finals, and the winner is crowned the Stanley Cup champions.

The Las Vegas Knights took home the Stanley Cup in the 2022-2023 season, while the Montreal Canadiens hold the title for the most championships (24). The Toronto Maple Leaves are right behind them with 13 and the Detroit Red Wings have 11.

So where can hockey fans stream NHL games? Read on for how to watch NHL games live for free to see which two teams make it to the 2024 Stanley Cup Finals.

How to watch NHL games live for free

NHL games air on ABC, TNT, and ESPN, which are available to stream on services like DirecTV Stream, Fubo and Sling Orange + Blue. DirecTV Stream offers a five-day free trial, starts at $74.99 per month after the trial ends (with $10 off your first three months), and includes ABC, ESPN, ESPN2, and TNT. and Fubo offers a seven-day free trial, which starts at $74.99 per month after the trial ends, and includes ABC, ESPN, ESPN2, and TNT. Sling Orange + Blue doesn’t offer a free trial, however, it is cheaper per month than the other options. Sling Orange + Blue starts at $30 for your first month and $60 after the first month ends. You’ll be able to watch ABC, ESPN, ESPN2, and TNT.

Some games are also available on ESPN Plus, which costs $9.99 per month for a monthly plan or $99.99 per year for a yearly plan. This option also lets you watch games that are on ABC and it’s the best one for out-of-market fans since it has NHL Center Ice.
