Historical Background

Rob Roy is a 1995 historical drama film set in 18th century Scotland, during a time of political unrest and clan feuds.

Based on True Events

The movie is loosely based on the life of the Scottish folk hero Robert Roy MacGregor.

Starring Liam Neeson

Liam Neeson portrays the title character, Rob Roy MacGregor, a respected Highlander who becomes embroiled in a conflict with an aristocrat.

Jessica Lange’s Performance

Jessica Lange delivers a powerful performance as Rob Roy’s wife, Mary MacGregor, who stands by her husband’s side throughout the challenges he faces.

Iconic Villain

Tim Roth plays Archibald Cunningham, the film’s antagonist and one of the most memorable villains in movie history.

Epic Sword Fights

The movie features intense and well-choreographed sword fights that showcase the skill and bravery of the characters involved.

Breathtaking Scottish Landscapes

The stunning Scottish landscapes provide a visually appealing backdrop for the film, capturing the rugged beauty of the Highlands.

Powerful Score

The film’s score, composed by Carter Burwell, adds depth and emotion to the storytelling, enhancing the scenes and capturing the essence of the characters.

Critically Acclaimed

Rob Roy received positive reviews from critics, who praised the performances, storytelling, and attention to historical detail.

Box Office Success

The film was a moderate box office success, earning over $31 million worldwide.

Accurate Costumes

The costumes in Rob Roy were meticulously designed to reflect the fashion and style of the 18th century.

Political Intrigue

The movie delves into the political turmoil of the time, with themes of oppression, betrayal, and the struggle for justice.

Filmed on Location

Many scenes in the movie were shot on location in the Scottish Highlands, adding an authentic feel to the film.

Historical Accuracy

A Tale of Honor

Rob Roy explores the concept of honor and the lengths one man will go to protect his family, clan, and dignity.

Supporting Cast

Award-Winning Performance

FAQs

Q: Is Rob Roy based on a true story?

A: Yes, Rob Roy is based on the life of the real Scottish folk hero, Robert Roy MacGregor. While the film does take some creative liberties, it is inspired by historical events and the legends surrounding Rob Roy’s life.

Q: Who directed Rob Roy?

A: Rob Roy was directed by Michael Caton-Jones, a renowned Scottish director known for his work in films such as Scandal, The Jackal, and The Man Who Knew Too Little.

Q: When was Rob Roy released?

A: Rob Roy was released in 1995. It received critical acclaim and was nominated for several awards, including an Academy Award for Best Actor for Liam Neeson’s outstanding performance.

Q: Where was Rob Roy filmed?

A: Rob Roy was primarily filmed on location in Scotland, capturing the majestic beauty of the Scottish Highlands. Some scenes were also filmed in Ireland and England.

Q: What is the running time of Rob Roy?

A: The running time of Rob Roy is approximately 139 minutes.

Q: Can children watch Rob Roy?

A: Rob Roy is rated R for violence and brief sexuality. Parents should use their discretion in determining whether the film is suitable for their children.
