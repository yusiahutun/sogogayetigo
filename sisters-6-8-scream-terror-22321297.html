Sisters, 6 and 8, scream in terror as beloved pooch left bloody in savage attack

March 2024 · 3 minute read

Two young sisters were left horrified and screaming after they saw their beloved labrador being attacked by another dog at a beauty spot.

Their horrified dad Rob Hinton was out walking with their labrador Maggie, with his daughters, aged eight and six, at Foremark Reservoir, near Repton, when the 15-month-old pet was attacked by another dog.

It happened it the Carver's Rocks area of the visitor attraction on Wednesday, July 1, when an older black labrador, which was off its lead, started growling at Maggie, who was also off her lead. The black dog bit

Maggie's ear making pour with blood, said Mr Hinton.

The 45-year-old, from Repton, regularly takes Maggie on walks around the reservoir.

He told DerbyshireLive : "It was an unnerving experience, especially having my daughters with me – they were very distressed.

"I kept thinking after why did I do this and that but I had my 15-month-old lab and my daughters at Foremark and I wanted to get them away and get them home.

"If they weren't with me there are lots of things I would've done differently.

"My daughter screamed and blood was pouring from Maggie's ear so I just wanted to get them in the car and back to their mum.

"It was very unpleasant."
