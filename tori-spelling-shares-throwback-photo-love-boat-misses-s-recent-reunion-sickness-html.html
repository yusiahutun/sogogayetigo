Tori Spelling shares throwback photo from The Love Boat

August 2024 Â· 3 minute read

One of her first TV roles was on the hit 1970s series The Love Boat.

And on Wednesday, Tori Spelling recalled fond memories from the show when she shared on Instagram a throwback photo while she was a guest star on the drama.

The post revealed that the 41-year-old actress wasn't always a blonde. 

Scroll down for video 

Throwback to the good days: Tori Spelling shared this vintage photo on her Instagram account on Wednesday in lieu of her duties as master of ceremonies aboard The Love Boat cast reunion on Princess Cruise's new vessel

Blonde beauty: The True Tori star left her brunette days behind having sported her lighter locks for many decades now

Spelling relayed that due to illness, she wasn't able to attend the cast reunion aboard the brand new Princess Cruises ship in Florida which was held on the same day.

In 1983, almost 31 years ago, Tori made several cameos on The Love Boat, which her late father Aaron served as executive producer.

In honor of the debut sailing of the Regal Princess vessel, the original cast and slew of guest stars gathered on the large cruise boat for a reunion and ship christening ceremony.

Regal Princess: The 41-year-old actress was scheduled to attend the christening ceremony of the cruise ship, pictured above, but missed the event due to her recent week-long hospitalization

Original cast reunited: The cast members from the hit 1970s - 1980s television series gathered for a photo op during the show's reunion

The True Tori star was scheduled to serve as master of ceremonies for the event but was unable to make it after her recent week-long hospitalization.

In lieu of her appearance, Tori posted a vintage snap from the show on her social media pages along with the message: 'Sad to be missing the #LoveBoat reunion on @PrincessCruises new #RegalPrincess. Those were amazing times! #DadWouldBeProud.'

Brady Brunch actress Florence Henderson replaced her hosting role in the midst of Tori's recovery from severe bronchitis, sinusitis and pneumonia.

The former Beverly Hills, 90210 star also shared a photo of the new 3,560 passenger ship writing, 'wishing @PrincessCruises and the entire Love Boat cast a wonderful reunion and christening on #RegalPrincess.'

Health scare: The star was kept in a LA hospital after diagnosed with severe bronchitis, sinusitis and pneumonia

Last minute replacement: The Brady Brunch actress Florence Henderson replaced Tori as the night's host

At the event, original castmembers Gavin MacLeod, Fred Grandy, Ted Lange, Bernie Kopell, Lauren Tewes and Jill Wheland reunited and were named godparents to the cruise ship.

The TV series and boating company had worked together throughout the show's ten year run in which every episode showed passengers, played by guest actors, travel from Los Angeles to Mexico aboard the cruise named the Love Boat.

Several of Princess Cruise's vessels were used as the show's setting even filming the pilot episode on the original Sun Princess.

The Love Boat: The romantic comedy show aired on the small screen from 1977 to 1987

During the reception, Henderson told the crowd, 'this ceremony honors the role that Princess Cruises ships played in the show. Tonight is going to be about love.'

The gathering was a 1970s get-together for many veteran TV stars including Loni Anderson, Joyce DeWitt, Don Most, Marion Ross and Christopher Knight, whom were all in attendance.

Spanish-American entertainer Charo, who booked the most guest appearances on the show, also stopped by the event.

The Love Boat currently can be viewed as weekly re-runs on the TV Guide channel in the States.
