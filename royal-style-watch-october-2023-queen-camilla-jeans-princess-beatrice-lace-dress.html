Royal Style Watch: From Crown Princess Mary's most re-worn dress to Queen Letizia in satin

July 2024 · 25 minute read

As we enter the New Year we look forward to a wealth of royal style inspiration to come. The year has started strong with Crown Princess Mary wearing three dresses at three public outings in three days since Queen Margrethe's surprise abdication on New Year's Day. 

Queen Letizia looked so stunning when she stepped out to support Princess Leonor during her Military Easter debut.

The royals upped the style stakes over the festive season. December saw the royals arriving at Westminster Abbey to celebrate Princess Kate's annual Christmas carol concert at Westminster Abbey where the hostess seriously wowed in white.

The Princess also upped her game in a gorgeous Miu Miu cardigan during the VT of the televised concert. Zara Tindall looked so stylish in a burgundy coat and coordinating skyscraper heels. 

And who could forget the best looks of the ever-stylish royals of Spain, the Netherlands and Monaco? Queen Letizia rocked a power suit we can all take notes from, whilst Princess Charlene's Christmas card look was a masterclass in festive dressing.

Zara Tindall has also impressed with her array of winter coats this season but her recent figure-flattering suit has got to be one of her best yet. We can't wait to see which coats she takes into 2024.

Who's been on our Royal Style Watch so far this year? Keep scrolling to find out which of the royal set delivered in the style stakes.

Queen Letizia

Princess Leonor stepped out for a royal first on Saturday alongside her parents King Felipe VI and Queen Letizia for the Military Easter. The ever-stylish Queen was seen in a black maxi skirt which she styled with a dovetail grey satin blouse with a high neck and long sleeves. 

Letizia wore her hair off her face in a mid ponytail with a trendy front section swept over her face. She teamed the look with a pair of platformed black pointed-toe heels and put on a pair of glitzy flower earrings.   

Crown Princess Mary

Crown Princess Mary of Denmark looked so elegant when she stepped out to greet members of the diplomatic corps during a New Year reception at Christiansborg Palace in Copenhagen on Wednesday. 

The soon-to-be Queen wore a regal Julie Fagerholt Handmade satin floor-length gown that featured a high Mandarin collar. The gown also featured a figure-flattering mermaid silhouette and padded shoulders, as well as fitted long sleeves. 

She teamed the look with a pair of Jimmy Choo 'Romy 100' navy suede pumps with a stiletto heel. Mary was also seen holding a navy calfskin leather clutch by Quindam Bags. 

Princess Charlene

Princess Charlene of Monaco looked like a movie star when she was photographed with her husband Prince Albert for his New Year's speech. The Monaco royal wowed in a dark red Ralph Lauren velvet gown with long sleeves and an asymmetric neckline.

The ruching at the waist added a cinched touch. She accessorised the gown with gold accessories including a pair of dramatic glitzy earrings.

Crown Princess Mary

Crown Princess Mary stepped out in her most re-worn dress for the annual New Year's dinner at Amalienborg Palace. The royal looked every inch a future queen in her Birgit Hallstein burgundy velvet gown with a wide scooped neckline and long sleeves that she had worn six times before.

She wore an array of jewels including the Order of the Elephant and corresponding star insignia, as well as the Danish Ruby Parure Tiara, which is made up of sparkling leaves dotted with pink berries made of rubies.

Princess Kate

Princess Kate looked so elegant when she stepped out for the Christmas Day service at Sandringham. The royal was seen with members of the family including her own children and husband Prince William wearing a breathtaking bespoke Alexander McQueen coat dress with a cinched waist. 

She teamed the elegant piece with a pair of navy suede boots by Gianvito Rossi, one of her favourite brands, and added the 'Natasha’ Lake Blue Suede Clutch by Emmy London.

The finishing touch came in the form of a blue bow and arrow hat by Juliette Millinery and the diamond and sapphire drop earrings hat belonged to her late mother-in-law. 

Queen Camilla

The Queen looked so elegant when she stepped out on Christmas Day wearing neutrals for an understated look. The King's wife glowed in a beige form-fitting cashmere coat by Anna Valentine. To keep in with the neutral theme, Camilla opted for the 'Dressage’ knee-high boots by Russell and Bromley. 

Her Philip Treacy hat added a royal touch and she added depth to her look with a tan mini bag in a lizard print by Moynat.

Duchess Sophie

Duchess Sophie looked better than ever when she was seen heading into Sandringham Church with her husband Prince Edward. The royal wowed in a khaki coat dress by Claire Mischevani that featured a bow detail at the neck. 

She added an unusual touch for a royal - an array of animal print accessories. The Duchess wore a Sophie Habsburg clutch in a leopard and croc mixed print. Sophie also wore a pair of 'Love 85’ brown croc heels by Jimmy Choo and a Juliette Millinery fedora with a feather detail. 

Princess Anne

The Princess Royal looked elegant when she was seen on Christmas Day with her husband Timothy Laurence. The royal pair wore matching scarves, 'The Highgrove Heritage Scarf', designed by Highgrove and The Prince's Foundation that were a gift from King Charles. 

Anne paired her scarf with a white double-breasted coat dress and brown accessories in varying shades - a pair of taupe knee-high suede boots, leather gloves in a mahogany colour, and a chocolate-hued suede cross-body bag.

Zara Tindall

Princess Anne's daughter looked chic when she rocked a coat on Christmas Day. She was seen walking to the St Mary Magdalene Church wearing a gorgeous tailored emerald coat by Laura Green. The coat featured a belted waist and an oversized collar.

Zara teamed the coat with the 'Midi Mayfair' bag in champagne, green & black woven leather by Aspinal of London. The finishing touches were added in the form of a pair of black suede knee-high boots courtesy of Stuart Weitzman and a bow felted headband by Julitte Millinery.

Queen Letizia

The Spanish royal looked so glamorous when she attended the closure concert of the Spanish Presidency of the EU wearing a timeless Paco Rabanne black single-breasted velvet blazer with satin lapels and diamanté-encrusted buttons on the sleeves.

She teamed the blazer with matching trousers and a pair of pointed-toe kitten heels by Nina Ricci and the ‘Mary’ black satin panel flap style by Magrit. 

Princess Charlene

The Princess of Monaco rocked a daytime look when she attended the Christmas Tree at Monaco Palace celebrations with her husband Prince Albert . The royal wore a red cable knit sweater with a ribbed high neck under a navy blue longline coat. 

The star of the show was her cherry red suede boots with a skinny kitten heel which she teamed with a coordinating pair of wool gloves. She stepped outside the royal box with an orange-toned lip that was so festive.
