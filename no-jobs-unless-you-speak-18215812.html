No jobs...unless you speak Polish

May 2024 · 2 minute read

Cooked meat giant Forza AW advertised for Polish-speaking employees to ensure they could understand instructions.

Critics claim it bars anyone except Poles from getting a job on the East Anglia-based production line.

Forza’s advert was sent out via email by employment agency OSR Recruitment earlier this month and dispatched to hundreds of potential applicants on the agency’s books.

It was signed by Katrina Massingham, the firm’s industrial team leader. The advert read: “Ongoing factory work (meat production) for 4-5 months, shifts are 7am-5pm or 9am-7pm. Transport provided. Applicants must speak Polish. Please call asap!!!!!!”

A 31-year-old British job seeker who contacted OSR was told he had to be fluent in Polish to understand the health and safety training.

He said: “I couldn’t believe it when I first read it, are we in England or Poland for goodness sake?

“If it was a job where you were flying back and forth to Warsaw I could understand it, but you wouldn’t think language ability would be high on the list of requirements for someone packing sausages.”

A spokesman for the Government Equalities Office said that Forza’s insistence on Polish-speakers could be illegal under anti-discrimination legislation.

He said: “Unless there is a genuine need for a worker to speak a particular language it is against the law to require that they should do so as a condition of employing them.”

Shadow Immigration Minister Damian Green said the advert made a mockery of Government promises to create “British jobs for British workers”.

He said: “Gordon Brown must regret ever saying that because it has proved a cruel deception for millions of the unemployed.”

Forza AW is owned by businessman Max Hilliard, once described as “the most powerful man in the British pig industry”, and has a £140million turnover. It supplies sausages to several supermarket chains.

Mr Hilliard, 51, said the advert was a “breakdown in communications” between Forza and OSR.

He said: “We would never turn down an English person for a job on the basis that they didn’t speak Polish or any other language.

“It should never have happened and I apologise to anyone who was put off applying for jobs as a result of the email.”

As a temporary measure, the company leased factory space and machinery at Bernard Matthews Farms’ huge plant in Great Witchingham, just outside Norwich.

There is no other connection between the two firms.

Mario Bardwell, a director of OSR Recruitment, declined to comment on the advertisement.
