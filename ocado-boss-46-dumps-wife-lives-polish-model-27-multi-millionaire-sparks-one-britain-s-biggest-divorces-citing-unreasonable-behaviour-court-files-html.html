Ocado boss Tim Steiner dumps wife and lives with Polish model

The man who made millions transforming the way we shop has split from his wife and is now dating a Polish model two decades his junior.

Ocado boss Tim Steiner, 46, has set up home with Patrycja Pyka, 27, after separating from the mother of his four children following more than 14 years of marriage.

The marriage collapse has sparked one of Britain's biggest divorces over the multi-million-pound fortune he made from the online retail company, which helped revolutionise home deliveries of groceries.

Steiner (right) is understood to be living with Miss Pyka (left) in a large detached house on a private road around the corner from the £15million former marital mansion in Highgate, North London

The tycoon has filed for divorce from wife Belinda, citing unreasonable behaviour, according to a publicly-available decree nisi document seen by the Daily Mail.

But it is understood that legal wrangles continue over the terms of the settlement. Mr Steiner has hired leading divorce lawyer Fiona Shackleton, who represented Sir Paul McCartney in his bitter split from Heather Mills, according to a source who knows the couple.

In the meantime, he is understood to be living with Miss Pyka in a large detached house on a private road around the corner from the £15million former marital mansion in Highgate, North London, which was damaged in a fire two years ago.

Mr Steiner, who did not start his new relationship until the breakdown of his marriage, has been open about his girlfriend and was pictured proudly standing beside her at the launch of an exclusive Mayfair spa, Elemis, last year. But the divorce is said to have been tough for Mrs Steiner, 45, whom he married at Hampstead Garden Suburb Synagogue in North West London in 1999.

A source said: 'I don't get the impression Belinda has had a choice in getting divorced. Belinda and Tim got to know each other long before Ocado. She is from a respectable North London Jewish family from the Hampstead area. She was a very glamorous young woman and they met, got married and had children quite quickly.'

Mr Steiner went to Haberdashers' Aske's school in Elstree, Hertfordshire, before reading economics, finance and accountancy at Manchester University.

He worked as a bond trader for Goldman Sachs in London, New York and Hong Kong for eight years before quitting to launch Ocado in 2000.

The entrepreneur had the idea after reading about a grocery delivery company that went bankrupt in the US. He believed the concept would thrive in the UK, where the population density is far higher.

Steiner has filed for divorce from wife Belinda (pictured), 45, citing unreasonable behaviour

Mr Steiner set up Ocado with Jonathan Faiman – with whom he had attended nursery school – and fellow Goldman Sachs banker Jason Gissing.

They each took a 90 per cent salary cut and raised the money from venture capitalists, friends and relatives as they initially went into partnership with Waitrose.

Ocado now claims to be the world's largest dedicated online grocery retailer, with more than 500,000 regular customers.

After announcing that the company would float on the stock exchange in 2010 – despite failing to have made a pre-tax profit at that point – Mr Steiner admitted that he was facing difficult questions at work and at home. He told an interviewer: 'It's so difficult at the moment, everyone wants to ask me questions, even my wife is asking me. I don't know if I can even answer her.'

Mr Steiner usually left the company's public face to Mr Gissing, an Oxford graduate who was famously pictured in the same Bullingdon Club photograph as Chancellor George Osborne.

The arrangement continued even after Mr Gissing jokingly suggested to an interviewer that he had a better-looking wife than Mr Steiner.

As well as the Highgate mansion, the Steiners have a holiday chalet in the Three Valleys in France, where the family used to spend long weekends and holidays skiing. Mr Steiner once said: 'I've got two preoccupations outside work. Spending as much time as possible with my four children and skiing.'

In the 2010 interview, he described how in his role as chief executive he woke at 7am each weekday and immediately checked the company's overnight figures. He was then chauffeur-driven to Ocado's base in Hatfield, Hertfordshire, where he worked until 7pm, dealing with more than 300 emails a day before returning to his family.

He said: 'I don't do the retail social scene in the evening.'

Both Mr and Mrs Steiner declined to comment yesterday.
