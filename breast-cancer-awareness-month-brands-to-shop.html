23 Top Brands Supporting Breast Cancer Awareness Month in 2023

June 2024 · 8 minute read

In addition to menopause awareness, October also marks the arrival of Breast Cancer Awareness Month, an annual campaign that places an emphasis on the importance of regular screenings. While we highly encourage you to donate directly to organizations like the Susan G. Komen Fund, National Breast Cancer Foundation, Inc., The Pink Agenda, and others, a number of beauty and fashion brands, like Estée Lauder and Jimmy Choo, are also donating thoughtful portions of their sales through Halloween and beyond to anchor current and future breast cancer initiatives.

To help you narrow down a few ways to shop your favorite products while making a difference, we've curated a short list of companies pledging upward of 10 percent of their profits this month (and some through 2023) to breast cancer research.

Amika has maintained an ongoing partnership with the organization HairToStay—which works to fund scalp-cooling technology to cancer patients in need—since 2018. This year, in addition to donating 1 percent of all online sales from loveamika.com to HairToStay, amika has also released a limited-edition version of its beloved Soulfood Nourishing Mask, with $4 from each individual sale benefiting the charity.

Dana Donofree founded the bra label AnaOno after overcoming breast cancer when she was 27. AnaOno includes bras specifically designed for post-surgery, meeting an underserved need on the bra market, in addition to styles catering to a range of sizes and needs. The brand’s vital work caught ThirdLove’s attention, and for Breast Cancer Awareness Month, both labels are donating care packages to cancer patients and survivors through nonprofit Living Beyond Breast Cancer.
