American actor, James Spader is best known for portraying the eccentric character of 'Graham Dalton' in the film, "Sex, Lies, and Videotape". He won three Emmy's Awards and earned two Golden Globe Award nominations. Currently, he is busy with his crime drama series, The Blacklist. His other work includes Mannequin, The Homesman, Lincoln, Wolf, etc. He voiced the role character Ultron in the MCU's Avengers: Age of Ultron.

Early Life of James Spader

Born on February 7, 1960, in Boston, Massachusetts, USA, James Spader was born as James Todd Spader. He is the youngest child of Jean (Fraser) and Stoddard Greenwood "Todd" Spader. He is American and belongs to the White ethnicity. 

Spader has English, as well as smaller amounts of Scottish, French, Dutch, German, and Swiss-German, ancestry. He was raised along with his two sisters in a liberal and progressive surrounding.

James Spader attended The Pike School, where his mother taught art, and later joined Brooks School in North Andover, Massachusetts, where his father taught. He went to Phillips Academy but left in 11th grade to pursue his career in acting and he joined  New York’s ‘Michael Chekhov School'.

James Spader's Career 

James Spader started his acting career in 1981 with the major role in Endless Love. His first starring role was in Tuff Turf (1985) but he rose to stardom with his 1986 movie, Pretty in Pink where he portrays the character of Steff, a wealthy and arrogant playboy.

Spader appeared in various movies, Mannequin (1987),  Sex, Lies, and Videotape (1989),  Bad Influence (1990), Stargate (1994), Supernova (2000), The Stickup (2002), Shorts (2009), and many more. He also lends his voice to ‘Acura’ in several of its television commercials.

He won three  Emmy Awards for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series for his character as Alan Shore in The Practice and its spin-off Boston Legal.  He also succeeds in winning the  Satellite Award for Best Actor in a Series, Comedy, or Musical for the same role. 

Spader is part of the NBC series The Blacklist since 2013 where he played the role of the FBI's most wanted fugitive Raymond "Red" Reddington for which he earned two Golden Globe nominations.

In 2015, he was cast in the superhero action blockbuster film ‘Avengers: Age of Ultron’ as Ultron a villainous robot.
